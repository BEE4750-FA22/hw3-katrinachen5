There are multiple decision variables to consider. One decision variable is the installed capacity in MW of generator type g. Let this be $x_g$. Another decision variable is the production in MW from generator type g in period t. Let this be $y_{g,t}$. A third decision variable is considering the non-served energy in MWh in each period t. Let this be $z_t$.

The problem consisted of 6 different generator types: geothermal, coal, CCGT, CT, wind, and solar. A period is 24 hours, counting by hours (ie $t=1$ means 1 hour).

Let $C_g^{INV}$ be the investment cost for a generator g, $C_g^{OP}$ be the operating cost for a generator g, and $C_t^{NS}$ be the the non-served energy cost for time period t. Also, let $L_t$ be the length of the time period t. With these variables, we can model the cost as:

\[ minZ = \sum_g C_g^{INV} x_g + \sum_g \sum_t L_t C_g^{OP} y_{g,t} + \sum_t C_t^{NS} L_t z_t \]
